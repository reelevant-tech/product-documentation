# Why Reelevant — Writing Guidelines

## Target Audience
Two sub-personas:
1. **Business Prospect** — Decision-maker (CMO, Head of Marketing, VP Digital).
   Evaluating whether to purchase. Cares about: ROI, capabilities, ease of use.
2. **Tech Prospect** — Engineer tasked by the business to evaluate integration
   feasibility. Cares about: architecture, effort, maintenance, security.

## Tone & Style
- Professional, confident, benefit-oriented
- Avoid being "salesy" — let capabilities speak for themselves
- Use concrete numbers and specifics over vague claims
- For business: focus on outcomes ("reduce time-to-launch from weeks to hours")
- For tech: focus on facts ("99.9% uptime SLA, EU data residency")

## Vocabulary Rules
- Use ALL platform terms as canonical (Workflow, Datasource, Content, etc.)
- For business pages: briefly explain each term on first use within the page
  (e.g., "Workflows — the automation logic that decides what to show each user")
- For tech pages: use terms without explanation (assume they've read the overview)
- Never use purely internal jargon (Pinot, Pulsar, MongoDB) in business pages
- Tech pages CAN reference infrastructure choices at a high level

## Structure (Business pages)
- Lead with the problem being solved
- Show the outcome/benefit
- Use customer-relatable examples (retail, travel, finance)
- Include comparison tables where relevant
- End with clear CTA ("Talk to Sales", "See it in action")

## Structure (Tech pages)
- Lead with an architecture diagram or data flow
- Be specific about protocols, formats, and standards
- Document what DOESN'T require custom development
- Include integration effort estimates (time, team size)
- Link to developer-docs for full implementation details

## What NOT to include
- Platform UI instructions (link to product-guide)
- Code snippets longer than 5 lines (link to developer-docs)
- Pricing (that's on the website)
- Detailed internal technical architecture (Pinot, Pulsar internals)
